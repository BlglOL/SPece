Facts abut the Moon





It takes 27.3 days for the Moon to travel all

the way around the Earth and complete its orbit.



As the Moon travels, it rotates on its own axis, just like our planet.

It takes roughly the same amount of time for the Moon to make a full

rotation as it does for it to complete its orbit. This means we only

ever see around 60% of the Moon’s surface from Earth! The part that faces Earth

is known as the ‘near side’ and the other, the ‘far side’.

Its surface is home to mountains, huge craters and flat

planes called ‘seas’ made of hardened lava.



The temperature on the Moon varies from super hot to

super cold! When the Sun hits its surface, temperatures can

reach a scorching 127°C. But when the Sun ‘goes down’,

temperatures can plummet to around -153°C.



Scientists aren’t entirely sure how the Moon formed. A popular theory is that

a Mars-sized rock, named Theia, crashed into Earth around 4.5 billion years ago.

The debris from the collision clumped together to make what is now… Our Moon!
